Great Northern Bean Family Time Soup

When the kids were growing up, this was my "Sunday soup." Each Sunday afternoon it simmered on the stove for a couple of hours, filling the house with a tantalizing smell.

Here's what you will need:

  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 stalks of celery, chopped
  • 2 15.8-oz. cans of Great Northern Beans*
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tbsp. olive oil
  • (optional) 1/4 cup ham, pre-cooked and cut into quarter-inch cubes
  • large non-stick skillet with a lid
  • non-stick spatula
  • wooden spoon (for stirring and, if desired, for tasting)
* For those on low-sodium diets, we recommend Bush's brand reduced-sodium Great Northern Beans.

Directions:

  1. Warm the olive oil in the skillet.
  2. Add the garlic, onions and celery and cook on medium heat until soft.
  3. (optional) Add the ham and raise the temperature slightly to brown it.
  4. Add the beans and the water.
  5. Simmer for about two hours, stirring frequently, until everyone gets home.
  6. While stirring, use the spatula to scrape the bottom of the skillet so the soup doesn't stick and burn.
  7. Add water as necessary.
  8. Season and garnish to taste.
